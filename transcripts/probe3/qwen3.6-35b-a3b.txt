### Step 3: Combine using Chinese Remainder Theorem

Let $x = F(60)$.
$x \equiv 0 \pmod 8$
$x \equiv 45 \pmod{125}$

From the second congruence, $x = 125k + 45$ for some integer $k$.
Substitute into the first congruence:
$125k + 45 \equiv 0 \pmod 8$
$125 \equiv 5 \pmod 8$ (since $125 = 15 \times 8 + 5$)
$45 \equiv 5 \pmod 8$ (since $45 = 5 \times 8 + 5$)
So, $5k + 5 \equiv 0 \pmod 8$
$5(k+1) \equiv 0 \pmod 8$
Since $\gcd(5, 8) = 1$, we can divide by 5 (multiply by inverse of 5 mod 8, which is 5, since $5 \times 5 = 25 \equiv 1$):
$k+1 \equiv 0 \pmod 8$
$k \equiv -1 \equiv 7 \pmod 8$

So $k = 8m + 7$ for some integer $m$.
Substitute $k$ back into the expression for $x$:
$x = 125(8m + 7) + 45$
$x = 1000m + 875 + 45$
$x = 1000m + 920$

Thus, $x \equiv 920 \pmod{1000}$.

The remainder when $F(60)$ is divided by 1000 is 920.
